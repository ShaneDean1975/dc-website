Common Accidents in Pensacola

Pensacola's road network carries a mix of commuters, commercial trucks, military personnel, and tourists, creating conditions that lead to frequent and often serious collisions. Our attorneys handle accident cases on every major route in the area, including:

  • Interstate 10 (I-10) -- The primary east-west corridor through Pensacola sees high-speed collisions, multi-vehicle pileups, and commercial truck wrecks, especially during heavy rain and fog.
  • Highway 29 (North Palafox) -- A heavily traveled north-south route connecting Pensacola to Cantonment and Century with frequent intersection and rear-end accidents.
  • Davis Highway (US-29 South) -- One of the busiest commercial corridors in Pensacola with constant traffic and a high rate of distracted driving crashes near shopping centers.
  • Bayou Boulevard -- A congested residential and commercial road where speeding, red-light running, and pedestrian accidents are common.
  • Downtown Pensacola -- Narrow streets, heavy foot traffic, rideshare vehicles, and limited visibility contribute to car-on-car and car-on-pedestrian accidents in the downtown core.
  • Pensacola Bay Bridge and Three Mile Bridge -- Bridge-related crashes are often severe due to limited room to maneuver, high speeds, and exposure to crosswinds.

Whether your accident happened on a busy highway or a quiet neighborhood street, our team will investigate the circumstances, identify the responsible parties, and pursue every dollar of compensation you are owed.

Florida and Alabama Personal Injury Laws

Florida: Florida is a no-fault insurance state. Your own Personal Injury Protection (PIP) coverage pays for initial medical expenses regardless of fault. However, if your injuries are serious -- involving significant scarring, permanent injury, or disability -- you can file a claim directly against the at-fault driver. Florida applies a modified comparative negligence rule: your compensation is reduced by your percentage of fault, and you cannot recover if you are more than 50% responsible. The statute of limitations for personal injury in Florida is 2 years from the date of the accident.

Alabama: Alabama follows a strict contributory negligence rule, one of the most restrictive in the country. If you are found even 1% at fault for the accident, you may be completely barred from recovering compensation. Alabama's statute of limitations is also 2 years. Because of this harsh standard, having skilled legal representation is critical for any injury claim filed in Alabama.

Our firm is licensed in both states and understands how to navigate these different legal frameworks to protect your claim and maximize your recovery.

What to Do After an Accident in Pensacola

The steps you take immediately after an accident can significantly affect the outcome of your injury claim. We recommend the following:

  • Call 911 -- Report the accident and request medical attention, even if your injuries seem minor at first.
  • Document the scene -- Take photos of vehicle damage, road conditions, traffic signals, and any visible injuries.
  • Exchange information -- Get the other driver's name, insurance information, and license plate number.
  • Seek medical treatment -- Visit a doctor as soon as possible. Delayed treatment can hurt both your health and your claim.
  • Do not speak with the other party's insurance company -- Adjusters are trained to minimize your payout. Let your attorney handle all communication.
  • Contact Dean & Camper, P.A. -- Call us for a free consultation so we can begin protecting your rights immediately.

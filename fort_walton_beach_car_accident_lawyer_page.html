Common Accidents in Fort Walton Beach

The Fort Walton Beach area presents unique driving hazards that contribute to a high rate of accidents. Our attorneys have extensive experience handling cases involving crashes at the most dangerous locations in the region:

  • Highway 98 Collisions: US-98 is the primary east-west corridor through Fort Walton Beach, carrying heavy traffic volumes, commercial trucks, and tourists unfamiliar with the route. Rear-end crashes, T-bone collisions, and multi-vehicle pileups are common along this congested highway.
  • Beal Parkway (SR 189) Wrecks: Beal Parkway connects Fort Walton Beach to residential areas and sees high traffic during morning and evening commutes. Intersection accidents and distracted driving crashes are frequent along this corridor.
  • Eglin AFB and Hurlburt Field Area: Thousands of military personnel commute daily to Eglin Air Force Base and Hurlburt Field, creating heavy traffic on surrounding roads. Gate traffic backups and rushed commuters contribute to frequent collisions in these areas.
  • Mary Esther and Okaloosa Island: The bridge and roadways connecting Fort Walton Beach to Okaloosa Island experience seasonal congestion from beachgoers and tourists, leading to rear-end crashes and pedestrian accidents.
  • Tourist-Related Crashes: Visitors unfamiliar with local roads, distracted by GPS devices, or driving rental vehicles contribute to a significant number of accidents during peak tourist season from spring through fall.
  • Intersection Accidents: Dangerous intersections throughout Fort Walton Beach -- including Eglin Parkway at Hollywood Boulevard, Beal Parkway at Mary Esther Boulevard, and Racetrack Road at Beal Parkway -- are frequent sites of T-bone and left-turn collisions.

Florida and Alabama Injury Laws

Florida: Florida operates under a no-fault insurance system. Your own Personal Injury Protection (PIP) policy covers initial medical expenses regardless of who caused the accident. However, if your injuries meet the serious injury threshold -- including significant scarring, permanent injury, or disability -- you can pursue a claim directly against the at-fault driver for full compensation. Florida's statute of limitations for personal injury claims is 2 years from the date of the accident. Florida follows a modified comparative negligence rule, meaning your compensation is reduced by your percentage of fault, and you cannot recover damages if you are found more than 50% at fault.

Alabama: Alabama follows an at-fault liability system with one of the strictest negligence standards in the country: contributory negligence. Under this rule, if you are found even 1% at fault for the accident, you may be completely barred from recovering any compensation. Alabama's statute of limitations is also 2 years. Because of the contributory negligence doctrine, having an experienced attorney who can build a strong case establishing the other party's full liability is essential to protecting your claim.

Compensation Available in Fort Walton Beach Injury Cases

Victims of accidents in Fort Walton Beach may be entitled to recover compensation for the full extent of their losses, including:

  • Medical expenses -- emergency room visits, surgery, hospitalization, physical therapy, and ongoing medical care
  • Lost wages -- income lost while recovering from injuries, including paid time off and sick days used
  • Lost earning capacity -- diminished ability to earn a living due to permanent injuries or disability
  • Pain and suffering -- physical pain, emotional distress, anxiety, and mental anguish caused by the accident
  • Property damage -- vehicle repair or replacement costs and damage to personal belongings
  • Scarring and disfigurement -- permanent physical changes resulting from the accident or medical treatment
  • Punitive damages -- additional compensation in cases involving drunk driving, reckless behavior, or intentional misconduct

Types of Car Accident Cases We Handle

Our car accident attorneys represent victims in all types of motor vehicle collisions, including:

  • Rear-End Collisions - Often caused by tailgating, distracted driving, or sudden stops
  • T-Bone (Side Impact) Crashes - Common at intersections when a driver runs a red light or stop sign
  • Head-On Collisions - Among the most dangerous and deadly types of crashes
  • Multi-Vehicle Pileups - Complex crashes involving multiple drivers and insurance companies
  • Hit-and-Run Accidents - We help you pursue compensation even when the at-fault driver flees
  • Distracted Driving Accidents - Texting, phone use, and other driver distractions
  • Drunk Driving Accidents - DUI crashes that may warrant punitive damages
  • Uninsured/Underinsured Motorist Claims - When the at-fault driver lacks adequate coverage
  • Intersection Accidents - Collisions caused by failure to yield or running traffic signals
  • Rollover Accidents - Often involving SUVs and vehicles with a high center of gravity
  • Sideswipe Accidents - Caused by improper lane changes or merging
  • Rideshare Accidents - Crashes involving Uber, Lyft, and other rideshare services

Common Causes of Car Accidents

Understanding what caused your accident is critical for building a strong case. The most common causes of car accidents on the Gulf Coast include:

  • Distracted driving: Texting, eating, adjusting GPS, or any activity that takes attention from the road
  • Speeding: Exceeding the speed limit or driving too fast for road conditions
  • Drunk or impaired driving: Operating a vehicle under the influence of alcohol or drugs
  • Reckless driving: Aggressive driving, tailgating, weaving through traffic
  • Running red lights and stop signs: Failure to obey traffic signals
  • Fatigued driving: Drowsy driving, especially on long highway stretches
  • Weather conditions: Rain, fog, and wet roads are common hazards along the Gulf Coast
  • Defective vehicle parts: Brake failure, tire blowouts, steering malfunctions

Common Car Accident Injuries

Car accidents can cause a wide range of injuries, from minor to catastrophic:

  • Whiplash and neck injuries
  • Traumatic brain injuries (TBI) and concussions
  • Spinal cord injuries and paralysis
  • Broken bones and fractures
  • Internal organ damage and internal bleeding
  • Herniated discs and back injuries
  • Burns and scarring
  • Soft tissue injuries (sprains, strains, tears)
  • Post-traumatic stress disorder (PTSD)
  • Wrongful death

Florida and Alabama Car Accident Laws

Florida: Florida is a no-fault insurance state, meaning your own Personal Injury Protection (PIP) covers initial medical costs regardless of who caused the accident. However, if your injuries meet the serious injury threshold -- significant scarring, permanent injury, or disability -- you can step outside no-fault and file a claim against the at-fault driver. Florida's statute of limitations for car accident injury claims is 2 years from the date of the accident. Florida follows a modified comparative negligence rule, meaning your compensation is reduced by your percentage of fault, and you cannot recover if you are more than 50% at fault.

Alabama: Alabama is an at-fault state with a contributory negligence rule -- one of the strictest in the nation. If you are found even 1% at fault for the accident, you may be barred from recovering any compensation. Alabama's statute of limitations is 2 years from the date of the accident. Because of the contributory negligence rule, having an experienced attorney is critical to protecting your claim.

Compensation Available in Car Accident Cases

Victims of car accidents may be entitled to recover:

  • Medical expenses - Emergency treatment, surgery, hospitalization, rehabilitation, and future medical care
  • Lost wages - Income lost while recovering from your injuries
  • Lost earning capacity - Reduced ability to earn a living in the future due to permanent injuries
  • Pain and suffering - Physical pain, emotional distress, and mental anguish
  • Property damage - Vehicle repair or replacement costs
  • Loss of enjoyment of life - Inability to participate in hobbies and activities
  • Scarring and disfigurement - Permanent physical changes from the accident
  • Punitive damages - Additional damages in cases involving drunk driving or extreme recklessness
  • Wrongful death damages - Funeral expenses, lost financial support, and loss of companionship
